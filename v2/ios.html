Cantonese on iPhone, iPad, iOS

How to write, type, listen, dictate, and use Siri in Cantonese with your Apple iPhone or iPad.

Write

To input Chinese by Handwriting or strokes:

  1. Go to Settings > General > Keyboard > Chinese (Traditional)
  2. Select your preferences:
    • ‘Handwriting’ (繁體手寫)
    • ‘Stroke’ (繁體筆畫)
    • ‘Cangjie’ (繁體倉頡)

Type

Typing in Cantonese Chinese (Yale, Jyutping) is not yet officially supported on iOS. Only Mandarin (Pinyin, Zhuyin, Shuangpin) is currently supported.

However, Third party keyboards can be used to type Cantonese on an iPhone or iPad. These include (in no particular order):

  • 迪迪輸入法 - 廣東話輸入法
  • 廣東話鍵盤
  • 廣東話輸入法
  • 中文輸入法 - 倉頡、速成、廣東話輸入法
  • CantoKey 粵拼輸入法

Dictate

To input Cantonese Chinese by speaking to your iPhone (Speech to Text):

  1. Go to Settings > General > Keyboard
  2. Ensure “Enable Dictation” is On
  3. Select Dictation Languages
  4. Select Cantonese

Now, wherever you see the keyboard, you can hold the Dictation button (  ) and select Cantonese (粵語). You can also hold the Globe button ( 🌐 ) when dictating to change languages.

Note: You must have a default Chinese input method, before being able to dictate in Cantonese (see “Write”).

Listen (TTS)

To get your iPhone to read out Cantonese Chinese text (Text To Speech):

  1. Go to Settings > General > Accessibility > Speech, and turn on “Speak Selection”. This will show a Speak button when text is selected.
  2. (Optional) You can also turn on “Speak Screen”. This allows you to hear the content of the screen when you swipe down with two fingers from the top of the screen.
  3. Under Voices > Chinese, select "Chinese (Hong Kong)"

Now you can get your iOS Device to speak Cantonese by highlighting text and selecting Speak.

Siri

  1. Go to Settings > Siri & Search > Language and select “Chinese (Cantonese - Hong Kong)”

Now you can use Siri in Cantonese.

Footnotes

Note: This document is written for devices running iOS 11 or higher. Instructions vary for older devices.

  • To find the iOS version installed on your device, go to Settings > General > About.
  • If you are not running iOS 11, you can update it wirelessly or through iTunes.

Note: For bilingual users, iOS11 has separated Siri, Dictation, and Region from each other - allowing for better bilingual usage (e.g. English and Cantonese).
